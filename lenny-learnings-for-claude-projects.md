## Leadership & Psychology (Dr. Becky Kennedy)

**Repair > Perfection**
Secure relationships are built on repair cycles, not perfection. Mess up → acknowledge → reconnect. At work: "Earlier I cut you off. I disagree but that tone was wrong. Working on it. Sorry." Trust resets faster than pretending it didn't happen.

**Most Generous Interpretation (MGI)**
Replace "they're being difficult" with curiosity: "Did they not feel heard? Are they stressed?" You cannot be judgmental AND curious simultaneously — one cancels the other. One MGI conversation fixes what endless venting won't.

**Sturdy Leadership ≠ Democratic**
Teams don't want consensus voting. They want a pilot who makes hard calls confidently. Sturdy: "This will be hard, I see it, we'll get through it." Weak: "Do we all agree?" (gives power to worst performer in crisis).

**Boundaries = Actions You Control**
Wrong: "Don't do X [or consequence]." (Gives power away; you'll break the threat.)
Right: "I will [action that removes the option]." You own the outcome. Prevents "job confusion" where you ask others to do your job.

## Hiring (Brian Halligan — HubSpot founder, Sequoia CEO coach)

**100% Attrition on Big-Company Hires**
Microsoft/Google folks at high-growth startups: 100% churn. Startups need adaptable operators, not bureaucratic excellence.

**Spiky > Smooth Candidates**
Mixed strong reactions (some rate 4/4, others 2/4) outperform uniform 3/4 ratings. Polarizing = distinctive.

**Blind References Are Everything**
Ask: "Would you rehire them?" and "Were they top 1%?" These questions signal honesty. VCs are best at references because they ask hard questions.

**Interview Hack: Send Board Deck First**
Share confidential deck/memo under NDA, then have 30-min discussion. Red flag: "This is amazing, great work." You want people who challenge you.

**CEO Scaling Bottleneck**
Going from <100 to 100+ employees = 50% of CEO time on hiring/interviewing C-suite. Expect ~2 C-level turnovers per year. This is normal.

## Growth & Product Frameworks

**Jason Cohen's 5-Question Diagnostic (order matters)**
1. Are customers leaving? (logo churn) — fix first
2. Pricing too low? (raise and measure)
3. NRR declining? (unit economics)
4. Marketing saturated? (find new channels)
5. Do you actually need to grow? (not everyone does)

First broken link breaks everything downstream.

**Churn Math (Jason Cohen)**
Max customers = new customers/month ÷ churn rate. At 100 new + 5% churn, ceiling is 2,000 forever. Cancellations grow exponentially while marketing grows linearly. Marketing is optional; churn-fixing is mandatory.

**Column B Thinking (Melanie Perkins, Canva — $42B)**
Column A: Build from available bricks. Column B: Dream of the castle, work backward.
Canva rejected 100+ investors but stayed obsessed with the vision. Rejections became pitch deck improvements, not pivot triggers. Goals may take 2x longer than planned — celebrate milestone rungs.

**AI as Tutor > AI as Worker (Marc Andreessen)**
Most people ask "What can AI do for me?" Equally powerful: "What can AI teach me?"
Bloom's 2-Sigma effect: one-on-one tutoring raises outcomes from 50th to 99th percentile. AI makes this economically viable. Spend spare hours in learning mode: "Train me to be a PM," "Make me assignments, evaluate results."

## AI & Building (Actionable Workflow Insights)

**Engineers as Tech Leads of Agent Fleets (Sherwin Wu, OpenAI)**
ICs becoming managers of 10-20 parallel AI agent threads. Hidden stress: when agents break, engineers lose their "escape hatch" of rolling up sleeves. Teams maintaining 100% AI codebases discover this is where real problems emerge.

**Boris Cherny (Anthropic) — 10-30 PRs/Day**
100% of code written by Claude Code since November 2025. Running 5 agents simultaneously. Says he's never enjoyed coding more — removed all minutia.

**Multi-Model Code Review (Zevi Arnovitz, Meta)**
When non-technical: have Claude Code review its own code, then have Codex review it separately. Dual-model review catches subtle bugs neither alone would find.

## Megatrend vs. Hype (Jeetu Patel, Cisco)

Heuristic: if you need a PhD to explain it, it's not a megatrend. Real trends are explainable to anyone. AI is a megatrend (unlike crypto/Web3). Don't fight megatrends; don't succumb to vanity work around hype.
